# Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common types of cybercrime, where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. These attacks usually occur through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

A typical phishing incident begins when a user receives a message claiming there is an urgent issue, such as “your bank account will be blocked” or “suspicious activity detected.” The message contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website which looks almost identical to the real one. Without realizing it, the user enters their login credentials or financial details.

Students and young users are often targeted because they actively use digital platforms but may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. Once the attacker gets access, they can misuse the account, steal money, or even perform identity theft.

The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss and loss of personal data. These attacks mainly rely on creating panic or urgency, making users act quickly without thinking.

Awareness is the best defense. Users should always verify links, check sender details, and avoid sharing sensitive information online. Being cautious and alert can help prevent such cybercrimes effectively.
